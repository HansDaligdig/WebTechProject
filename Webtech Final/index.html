What is Career Development?

Career development describes a complicated process of planning activities and engagement in one’s career to establish growth in their chosen career (Serbes & Albay, 2017; Shaito, 2019). Such growth is valuable to both the individual and the company as it contributes to the progress of the individual in reaching their career objectives through the development of their skills, capabilities, decision-making, competence, and comprehension (Serbes & Albay, 2017; Saleem et al., 2013). It encompasses the need for understanding one’s career and its depictions as well as knowledge management and effort to achieve one’s personal desired future (Mulhall, 2014; Sinha, 2020).

Why having a Career Development is important?

Over the years, career development has become a key element that organizations seek to improve their performance and productivity (Nava-Macali et al., 2019, Saleem et al., 2019). Not only that, but career development is a notable factor in both employee satisfaction and retention (Hedge & Rineer, 2017; Nava-Macali et al., 2019). It affects the organization’s effectiveness as a whole, as career development enhances employee engagement, encourages open communication, and maximizes and improves employee skills, knowledge, and attitudes (McDaniels & Gysbers, 1992 as cited in Chu et al., 2007; Nava-Macali et al., 2019; Serbes & Albay, 2017; Shaito, 2019; Sinha, 2020).

Statistics

A worker's emotional dedication to a company and its goals is known as employee engagement. Companies can increase employee retention by providing career development opportunities, encouraging organizational loyalty, and supporting workers. By implementing these tactics, companies can improve the work environment and increase employee happiness levels, ultimately improving employee retention rates. According to employee training statistics, 74% of employees are willing to take a retraining course or learn more skills to keep their current position and 70% of workers will at least be inclined to change their current employer into one that prioritizes employee learning and development.
