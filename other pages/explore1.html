How to Build an Eye-Catching Resume

Adrienne Zapanta

26/08/2023

Being able to build an eye-catching resume should be your goal to stand out among the crowd in any company that you are going to apply to. You should be able to make a solid impression on your employers by starting with creating your resume. It is time for you to level up! So, here are some of the tips that you could use in creating a unique but at the same time eye-catching resume.

Tip #1: Include relevant information

When building a resume you should focus on including the significant information that you need when creating a resume. Include information such as your work experiences which are over the past 10 years. Include only your 5 to 10 best skills alongside the most recent accomplishments you have done while performing your work.

Tip #2: Use the correct format in creating your resume

To be able to build an eye-catching resume you must consider following an updated format in building your resume. Use a layout or template that would make it easier for your employers to read. Do not create a resume that takes too much time to read.

Tip #3: Your resume must consist of keywords

Including keywords in your resume makes it easier for your reader to identify significant information about you. Include short phrases which are related to the job position you are applying for. Including keywords helps your recruiter to easily recognize you.

Tip #4: Build a unique career objective/ goal

Write a career objective it states your significant skills and abilities as well as the significant accomplishments that you have done in your previous job. Make it simple and short but it must be unique and at the same time strong to give your employer an idea of the things that you can do and offer to them as an employee.

Tip #5: Always double-check your resume

Before submitting your resume always make sure that it is concise and that you have followed the standard and updated format in building your resume. Check all the things you have included, especially the grammar, the structure, and the context of your resume. Make sure to double-check or even triple-check your resume and have someone proofread it first or use a proofreading application just to be sure.
