Tips and Tricks for Your Next Interview

Adrienne Zapanta

26/08/2023

Does anyone like going to interviews? Having to sit across someone as they come and judge your every move and evaluate everything you say more often than not causes us to be riddled with anxiety and nervousness as we work to give the best impression. And that’s normal, especially when your career depends on it. But how exactly can you improve on how you handle these interviews in the future? Well, here are a few tips and tricks to help you with that.

Tip #1: Dress Smart and Carry Yourself Properly

Dressing appropriately for the job you’re trying to apply for shows the employer that you put care into how you present yourself to others, especially in a professional setting. However, that is only half the job. Exuding confidence in your posture, body language, and gestures gives the impression that you are a poised person who is prepared for the said interview.

Tip #2: Set Your Game Plan

Getting ready for your interview is not something you can do in under an hour. Preparing for the interview includes an interview plan. First and foremost, you should be familiar with what you wrote in your resume, what the job qualifications are, and the company you are applying for. Next, prepare some mock questions that you believe will be asked by your interviewer. Practice what you are going to say and make sure that you stay honest with those answers. Finally, make sure that you have your clothes prepared and that you get a good night’s rest the day before and eat a heavy meal to ensure that you are attentive and energized for the interview.

Tip #3: Mind Your Manners

From the moment you step into the interview place, make sure to practice proper courtesy and politeness when greeting your interviewer and any other people that you may encounter during the interview process. This includes proper greeting, being punctual, saying thank you, and in some cases, even bowing. That’s why make sure that you try to research your company and see their basic work etiquette.

Tip #4: Be Honest and Yourself

If there’s one thing that’s a turn-off for interviewers, it’s applicants who are faking it to make it to the top. Now, we always want to give a good and lasting impression on our interviewers, but you must always be the best version of yourself. Showing your genuine self in your actions and words will let the interviewer know what your true intentions are and it will gratefully impact the flow of your interview.

Tip #5: Listen!

Here’s where that good night’s sleep is going to get into play. During your interview, always listen attentively to your interviewer. Asking questions like “Can you repeat the question?” or “I’m sorry, what was that?” will give the impression that you do not care and are not interested in what they have to say. So always make sure to listen; sit up straight and maintain good eye contact.

Tip #6: Emphasize Your Strong Points and Get Ready for Questions About Your Weaknesses

Just like in every employer, they are seeking the strongest candidates for the job. However, no candidate is perfect, and they all have varying skills and weak points. Think of it as though you’re advertising a product. As an interviewee, you must always point out what you excel in and what you can bring to the table if they choose to hire you. It is inevitable that they will also ask you about what skills you don’t have or lack in. To deal with this question, you must frame that weakness positively. Say that it is a challenge that you are willing to improve upon through training and continuous effort to transform that weakness into a strength.

Tip #7 Draw the Final Curtain Politely

Just like with the positivity you showed when the interview started, you must also bring the same energy at the end of the interview. Reiterate your strong points, thank them for their time, and bid them goodbye with a handshake and a smile.

With these tips, hopefully, it will aid you in acing your upcoming interview or prepare you for the time you will. Just a little bit of effort on your part as the interview will get you one step closer to getting that job. Good luck!
