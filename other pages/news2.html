Are Hybrid Work Set-ups About to Replace Traditional Ones?

Adrienne Zapanta

26/08/2023

Imagine a typical work life. You wake up early, take the morning commute, get stuck in traffic, and go to your work cubicle until the end of your shift. That was how we were shown the usual work setup. But with the introduction of technology in the workplace, work shifts have shifted (pun intended) to including online or work from home for their employees’ work schedules.

Such hybrid setups have grown in popularity, especially because of the COVID-19 pandemic. Because technology has become widely available, workers can now set up their own offices at home without sacrificing the quality of their work. It’s not something that companies are trying to avoid as well but is becoming more open to the idea of letting their employees adopt these flexible working arrangements.

What’s the appeal you might ask, and there’s a long list of reasons for that. Aside from the obvious reasons of flexibility and cost-effectiveness because of the lack of everyday commuting, a hybrid work set-up also helps with balancing one’s work life, a wider hiring pool, better mental health and time management, and higher employee satisfaction.

But just like with everything, it has its own downsides. For starters, not everyone has the means to set up their own offices at home. There is also a problem with collaboration and team rapport when employees don’t see each other as often. There is also a need for better maintenance and security, especially when work goes online and becomes vulnerable to outside threats.

However, even with these risks, a high number of job seekers are more attracted to the idea of working in a hybrid set-up, with a survey finding out that almost half of them would actively seek another job should the one they’re applying for not offer it. Because of this, some surveys have found that up to 77% of companies have shifted to such a set-up and up to 80% of employees are advocating for their own companies to also take the leap and shift into this new set-up.

Now, although shifting is not as easy as it seems, it is not something that companies are avoiding. Another survey found that 66% of organizations have already fully shifted into this setup, and a whopping 30% of the organizations that didn’t are already actively shifting.

The question now is will it fully replace traditional work set-ups? Probably not, but with the recent application of technology in the workplace, it might just become the dominant work environment in the near future. Only time will tell if this will continue to evolve and spread its influence in the workplace.
