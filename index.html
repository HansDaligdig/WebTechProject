What is Career Development? Career development describes a complicated process of planning activities and engagement in one’s career to establish growth in their chosen career (Serbes & Albay, 2017; Shaito, 2019). Such growth is valuable to both the individual and the company as it contributes to the progress of the individual in reaching their career objectives through the development of their skills, capabilities, decision-making, competence, and comprehension (Serbes & Albay, 2017; Saleem et al., 2013). It encompasses the need for understanding one’s career and its depictions as well as knowledge management and effort to achieve one’s personal desired future (Mulhall, 2014; Sinha, 2020). Photo by Unplash
Why having a Career Development is important? Over the years, career development has become a key element that organizations seek to improve their performance and productivity (Nava-Macali et al., 2019, Saleem et al., 2019). Not only that, but career development is a notable factor in both employee satisfaction and retention (Hedge & Rineer, 2017; Nava-Macali et al., 2019). It affects the organization’s effectiveness as a whole, as career development enhances employee engagement, encourages open communication, and maximizes and improves employee skills, knowledge, and attitudes (McDaniels & Gysbers, 1992 as cited in Chu et al., 2007; Nava-Macali et al., 2019; Serbes & Albay, 2017; Shaito, 2019; Sinha, 2020).
